$\delta 4.3$ Moments

Defn The r-th moment about the ongin of a r.v. $X$, denoted by $\mu_{r}^{\prime}$ is $E\left(X^{r}\right)$, the expected value of $X^{r}$. In the discrete case
$$ \mu_{r}^{\prime}=E\left(x^{r}\right)=\sum_{x} x^{r} \cdot f(x) $$
and in the ots case
$$ \mu_{r}^{\prime}=E\left(x^{r}\right)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x^{r} \cdot f(x) d x $$
n.b usually (nearly always), $r$ is a non-negative integer ie $r=0,1,2 \ldots$
N.b. the analogy with moments of inertia in physres, whence the name.
When $r=0, \quad \mu_{0}^{\prime}=E\left(X^{0}\right)=E(1)=1$, by Car 2 of Thm 4.2.
When $r=1, \mu_{1}^{\prime}=E\left(X^{\prime}\right)=E(X)$, the expectation of $X$. This is also called the mean of the r.v. $X$,
Defn. $\mu_{1}^{\prime}$ is called the mean of the distribution of $X$, or simply the mean of $X$ and it is denoted by $\mu$.
Of importance to us are the following.
Defn. The rth moment about the mean of a r.v, $X$, denoted by $\mu r$ is the expected value of $(X-\mu)^{r}$.
In the discrete case
$$ \mu_{r}=E\left((x-\mu)^{r}\right)=\sum_{x}(x-\mu)^{r} \cdot f(x) $$
and in the cts case
$$ \mu r=E\left((x-\mu)^{r}\right)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(x-\mu)^{r} \cdot f(x) d x $$
Again, usually $r=0,1,2, \ldots$.
Note that $\mu_{0}=1$ and $\mu_{1}=0$ fer any r.v. for which $\mu$ exists. $\mu_{2}$ is of special importence.
Defn. $\mu_{2}$ is called the variance of the distribution of $X$, or simply the variance of $X$ and it is denoted by $\sigma^{2}, \operatorname{var}(X)$, or $V(X) ; \sigma$, the positive square root of the variance is called the standard deviation
$\mu_{2}$ (or $\sigma$ ) measures how clocky the values of a r.v. are clustered around the mean.

Thm 4.6 $\quad \sigma^{2}=\mu_{2}^{\prime}-\mu^{2}$
Pf. $\quad \sigma^{2}=E\left((X-\mu)^{2}\right)$
$$ \begin{aligned} & =E\left(X^{2}-2 \mu X+\mu^{2}\right) \\ & =E\left(X^{2}\right)-2 \mu E(X)+E\left(\mu^{2}\right) \\ & =E\left(X^{2}\right)-2 \mu_{0} \mu+\mu^{2} \\ & =E\left(X^{2}\right)-\mu^{2} \\ & =\mu_{2}^{\prime}-\mu^{2} . \end{aligned} $$
