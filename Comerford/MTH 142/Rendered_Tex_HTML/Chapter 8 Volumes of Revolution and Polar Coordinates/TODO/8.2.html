§8.2 Applications to Geometry

Already saw a number of examples of how to use integration to find area/volume. In general, the approach is as follows.

To compute a volume/area/length using an integral:

  • Divide the solid/region/curve into small pieces whose volume/area/length we can easily approximate.
  • Add the contributions of the pieces together. This gives a Riemann sum which approximates the total volume/area/length.
  • Take the limit as the number of terms in the sum tends to infinity. This leads to a definite integral for the total volume/area/length.
