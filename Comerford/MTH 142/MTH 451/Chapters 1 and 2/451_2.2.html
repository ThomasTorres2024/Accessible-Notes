2.2 Sample Spaces

In probability and statistics, an observation or measurement is referred to as an experiment or trial.
The result of an experiment is called the outcome, and the set of all possible outcomes is called the sample space (usually referred to by S S SSS ).
Each outcome in the sample space is called an element of the sample space, or a sample point.
Sets of outcomes in the sample space are referred to as events.

Often the sample space is determined by the requirement of the problem.
Example. Roll a die.
If we want to know the exact outcome, we would choose the sample space
